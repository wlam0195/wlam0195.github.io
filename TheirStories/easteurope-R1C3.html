Fannie Sehoenholtz

from Russia to New York, USA

Their Stories

Their Journey

In 1866, Fannie Sehoenholtz and her husband, Nathan Glickman, sailed across the Atlantic Ocean to New York. They brought nothing but three suitcases. They would go on to open a shipping business and have eight children. Glickman would pass in January 1921, leaving the business to his daughters.

Where are they now?

Fannie Sehoenholtz's children had several granddaughters and a dog. She passed away at the age of ninety.
