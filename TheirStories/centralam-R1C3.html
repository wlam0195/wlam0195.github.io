Jose Auraz

from Cuba to California, USA

Their Stories

Their Journey

On August 13, 2009, Missoula YMAC offered permanent residency and a job for Auraz. He decided to cross into the United States by swimming through the Guantanamo Bay, the waters dividing the Cuban and US controlled territories. On November 20, 1993, after months of practicing, he successfully swam across. He only brought his ID card, swimsuit and waterproof wristwatch.

Where are they now?

As of August 2009, he works at an office at Poverello Center. He works as a laborer, a dishwasher, and a building farmer. He experienced two marriages, in which he had a son and a daughter. His first wife passed and second left, but he frequently visits his son in Missoula.
